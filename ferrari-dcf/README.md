## Investment Thesis

**April 2026 Update:** The Luce was officially unveiled on April 21, 2026, priced at ~€550,000 (~$649K at EUR/USD 1.18) — confirming the core ASP accelerant thesis. First deliveries are now set for October 2026, with full public reveal on May 25, 2026 in Rome. Q1 2026 earnings are scheduled for May 5, 2026 and represent the next near-term catalyst. Ferrari (NYSE: RACE) has pulled back **32.2% from its 52-week high of $519.10** — not because the business deteriorated, but because the market applied an EV-fear discount borrowed from legacy automakers. That discount is misplaced.

Four value creation drivers:

1. **The Luce EV Is an ASP Accelerant — Now Confirmed** — Ferrari officially unveiled the Luce on April 21, 2026, priced at ~€550,000 ($649K). This is roughly 2× the fleet average ASP, making it the most accretive vehicle in Ferrari history. Designed by Jony Ive, the Luce delivers 1,000+ hp via four motors, a 122 kWh battery, and October 2026 first deliveries. Unlike BMW or Mercedes, Ferrari's EV transition is a margin *expansion* catalyst, not a headwind.
2. **Multiple Re-rating Opportunity** — RACE trades at ~22× FY2026E EBITDA vs. Hermès at 30×. The correct peer is the luxury goods sector, not autos. A re-rating to 27× alone drives $498/share.
3. **FY2025A Confirms Acceleration** — Record 38.8% EBITDA margin, €7,146M revenue (+7.0% YoY), management guided FY2026E revenue of €7.5B and EBITDA of €3.0–3.1B (targeting 40%+ long-term margin). Q1 2026 results on May 5 will be the first print with Luce order data.
4. **Near-Debt-Free Balance Sheet + Structural Pricing Power** — Net industrial debt of only €180M (0.06× EBITDA). 24-month waitlists insulate revenue from macro — the company grew EBITDA through COVID.
